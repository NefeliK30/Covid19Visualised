Visualising Covid-19

As a Data Analytics graduate, I have decided to apply my knowledge in data visualisation in order to get an insight on the Coronavirus (Covid-19) outbreak around the world but more significantly in my home country, Cyprus.

The purpose of the post is only to visually illustrate data and not to implement or fit a predictive model on the current circumstances. This is due to the significance of the situation, the lock-down and social distancing measures applied by each country around the world in order to minimize the outbreak.

Data are provided by Johns Hopkins Coronavirus Resource Center (COVID19 Data), European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control (ECDC Data) and Press and Information Covid-19 in Cyprus (Cyprus Data).

All graphs include data up to the 27th of May 2020 and will be updated regularly to follow the progress of the pandemic.

The first graph, illustrates the whole’s world confirmed cases in a global map. This is an interactive Choropleth map created using plotly in python. Choropleth comes from the Greek words choros - "χώρος" meaning region and plethos - "πλήθος" meaning multitude. In Choropleth map each geographical region is shaded to represent statistical data. Hoovering over the map will indicate the total of confirmed cases in each country.

A more simplistic graph is shown below comparing the casualties and confirmed cases of Covid-19. From the graph it can be observed that the death rate of the virus is really low. Results of confirmed cases may differ and this is probably happening due to the small amount of people tested during the pandemic.

Let's have a look at the confirmed cases recorded in Cyprus. The highest confirmed cases recorded in the island was on the 1st of April 2020, where the cases reached 58. It can be seen that after that date the cases started to drop with some small increase during the weekends. The aim is to flatten the curve with few, or with the best case scenario, no casualties. Cyprus is trying to test as many people as possible in order to minimise the spread of the virus. The country took the first measures on the 13th of March and the complete lockdown was followed a few days later. Also, residents of some cities/municipalities are being tested in order to monitor the situation. Moreover, everyone arriving in the island with exceptions those traveled for medical reasons will remain in mandatory isolation (quarantine) for 14 days at premises indicated by the State. Another measure taken from the Republic of Cyprus is that anyone entering the country will be tested for Covid-19. The first day that the country recorded zero new cases was on the 23rd of May. Since the cases started to drop the country is now on the second phase of easing lockdown measures.

The population in the areas controlled by the State is around 876,000 and thus the island is trying to reduce the risk of spreading the virus by taking strict measures. As the Μinistry of Ηealth announced, Cyprus is the 3rd country in Europe with more than 4,000 tests/100,000 population.
